# School_District_Analysis

## Overview of the school district analysis 
##### The purpose of our analysis is to examine the data from a variety of different schools and breakdown how the students in these schools are performing on standardized tests. The anaylsis looks at both math and reading scores. It takes into account dollars spent per student in relation to scores, The type of school in questions (charter v. public), and the ages of the students. This analysis not only cleaned and organized the data, but it also removed any scores that were considered bad faith. This means that the final analysis removed all of the scores of the 9th graders from one high school in particular. 

## Results 
### How was the district summary affected after the bad faith scores were removed?
#### When the bad faith scores were removed: 
##### - The average math score went down by .1% 
##### - The average reading score remained the same 
##### - The percent passing math went down by .2% 
##### - The percent passing reading went down by .3% 
##### - The overall percent passing went down by .1%

### Summary 
#### The greatest impact that removing the bad faith scores had on the data can be observed in the results for Thomas High School. The greatest change occured in the District Summary, the  average and percent passing math and reading schools went down. In the school summary, we observed that the results for Thomas High School did change. The average math score went down by .1%. The percent passing math went down by .2%. The percent passing reading went down by .3% and The overall percent passing went down by .1%.
